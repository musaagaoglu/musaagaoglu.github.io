About

I am a PhD candidate in behavioral and experimental economics at GATE-Lyon and Université Lumière Lyon 2, supervised by Eugenio Verrina and Fabio Galeotti. I use field and laboratory experiments to study how people behave under social and organizational incentives.

My research fields are behavioral and experimental economics and microeconomics, with secondary interests in gender economics and organizational economics. Before Lyon, I completed an M.Sc. in Economics at the University of Copenhagen and a B.Sc. in Economics (Honors) at Middle East Technical University.

Research

Working paper
Signaling in Informal Contracts — A Field Experiment in Volunteer Management
Single author · draft in preparation, 2025
A field experiment (n = 683) at a large-scale event evaluating signaling mechanisms in volunteer management. Formal commitment significantly reduces no-shows and late cancellations but decreases overall shift completion; trust-based signals do not affect no-show rates but improve organizational efficiency by fostering earlier cancellations — highlighting the trade-offs between mandatory confirmation and relational signaling.
